About

I am drawn to work where materials decisions carry real consequences, where the gap between a good solution and a poor one is measured in performance, safety, or longevity. My research has centered on designing degradable polymer systems with tunable architecture, using characterization as a tool for iterative problem solving rather than just confirmation. I am most energized by complex, open-ended challenges that require reasoning carefully from first principles and refining toward a purpose-built answer. Open to roles in materials science and engineering, polymer science, R&D, and applications science in the Pacific Northwest and beyond.
